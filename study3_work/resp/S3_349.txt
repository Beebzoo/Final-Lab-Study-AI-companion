Well there have been many challenging interactions of late! One that immediately comes to mind is trying to organise care for my mum when she comes out of hospital. She has serious health conditions and following a bout of pneumonia, is keen to get home. Sadly she also has Alzheimer's but hides it well to those who don't know her, and insists that she doesn't need extra help! The social worker team have been speaking with mum directly and ignoring the fact that she is not deemed mentally capable or that any decisions need to be discussed with me. It's been very frustrating and not even knowing the names of the people concerned. So I started by speaking with the nurse in charge and obtaining as much information from them as possible. I then arranged a meeting with mum and myself at the hospital to talk things through with the social worker and the head nurse, who had also realised that mum had a type of Alzheimer's that meant she was very articulate and plausible but was clearly getting her facts mixed up; something that only came to light for them when the three of us were in the room together. With this support, as well as medical documentation confirming her diagnosis and validated by a brain scan, the social worker realised that mum had basically fooled them and that actually she did require help 3x a day and this is now being finalised before mum is able to return home.
