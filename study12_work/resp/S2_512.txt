Recently, I had a disagreement with a teammate during a group project. We had different ideas about how to divide the work. I wanted to plan everything out in detail, while they preferred to work more spontaneously. At first, it was frustrating because I felt like my suggestions weren’t being heard.

Instead of continuing to argue, I suggested we take a short break and then have a calm discussion about our priorities. When we talked again, I made sure to listen to their perspective before sharing mine. I realized that their approach actually helped keep the project flexible, while mine ensured we stayed organized.

We ended up combining our methods, creating a general plan but leaving room for creativity. It taught me that communication and compromise are key when working with different personalities.
