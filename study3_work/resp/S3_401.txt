A recent challenging interaction I had with someone and how I handled it was when I was a student in the university and I was living in the school dormitory. So, there was the issue about the emptying of the dustbin and the cleaning of the kitchen. So, my argument was that there must be a timetable whereby everybody would be involved on a periodic basis to make things more straightforward. But they were having the opinion that anybody around the kitchen at a point in time should be responsible for the cleaning. But that led to disagreement and issues because people were just leaving the dustbin. The dustbin was unemptied for days making the whole kitchen dirty, smelly and stinking until eventually we had to come to the reasonable ground that there must indeed be a timetable. So, I was very sure that having a timetable whereby every student in the flat would be involved in to contribute to the cleanness of the kitchen is the most reasonable way to go. This also drew from my past experiences living with other people. So, at the end of the day they were able to see reason to agree with me and a timetable was created whereby everybody was allocated slots to participate in the weekly cleaning of the kitchen which will include cleaning the kitchen, you know, the floor, the dining table, the chairs, those general cleaners of the kitchen and also arranging the kitchen and making sure that the kitchen looks tidy every week. So, at least because of our schedule the timetable was structured to be weekly so that every week which is only the weekend whoever is responsible will take up the responsibility.
