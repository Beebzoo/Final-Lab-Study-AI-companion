I recently had a difficult conversation with my wife about the fact that I've been out of work for 2026. However, just being out of work was not the issue. The challenging portion was that she was concerned about the way I was spending as of late on leisure items such as playing golf and a poker game with friends. We obviously are on a one-income currently, which is hers, and I am supplementing that with some online surveys. So, it was definitely a challenging interaction because it first came up while I was in the middle of the poker game. So, that didn't help my focus. However, I had to hear her out and I fully understood where she was coming from. I also let her know that while I completely agree that we need to cut down on any unnecessary spending or bad habits, that if we have advance notice that we are doing something, then we should talk about it beforehand and not wait until the day of or even while we're doing a certain event to discuss whether it's a good choice to be doing it or not. All in all, it was a productive interaction, one that I was not expecting or that I felt too comfortable with at the beginning. But the basis of a solid marriage is to be able to not take things too hard personally and be able to understand each other's sides. I think that we accomplished both. We have a good understanding of what we want to do over the next few months if I continue to be out of work. And overall, I came out of it with a positive feedback and was glad that it actually came up.
