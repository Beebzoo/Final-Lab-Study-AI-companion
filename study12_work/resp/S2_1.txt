A few months ago, when I was still studying in Spain, in biology class we had to do a group project on evolution according to Darwin. We were in a group of five, and there was one guy, Lucas Moreno, who never did anything. That is, he didn't prepare the project or the slides or collaborate in any way. My classmates and I were shocked to see how he had no intention of working in a group, so we tried to talk to him so he would help more with the project. He told us he was afraid to work because he didn't know anything about the subject; he was afraid of feeling out of touch with the subject. That's why I learned that you should never be guided by prejudices and you shouldn't be afraid to participate for fear of being judged by others.
