A recent challenging interaction that I had was somebody that I was managing was underperforming at work quite significantly. We put her on a pip and she refused to really take it very seriously. I think there was a bit of a misunderstanding as to how serious it was. We had regular meetings confirming progress and the risks of what would happen if she did not improve. Unfortunately, towards the end of that process, she did not improve. We put in a final judgement meeting to essentially tell her that she did not pass the pip. In between putting that meeting in and the meeting taking place, she resigned through a slack message, which was effective immediately. She did not understand that that meant she may not be entitled to her notice pay. But given the circumstances, we tried to work with HR to ensure that she would get that notice pay. I think that although she was not performing, it's the right thing to do. So, that's what we did.
