Recently, I had a challenging situation during a group project at university. One of my teammates wasn’t contributing equally, which created tension and delayed our progress. At first, I felt frustrated, but I decided to approach the situation calmly. I suggested a short meeting where everyone could share what they were struggling with. It turned out that the teammate was overwhelmed with other deadlines and didn’t know how to ask for help.

We redistributed some of the tasks and set clearer deadlines. After that, communication in the group improved a lot. From this experience, I learned the importance of empathy, transparency, and addressing issues early before they escalate. It also reminded me that good teamwork is built on understanding, not just dividing work.
