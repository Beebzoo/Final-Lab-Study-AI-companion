A few months ago, in high school, I had a disagreement with one of my teammates during a group project.  We had different opinions about how to divide the tasks. At first, I felt frustrated because i thought my ideas were not being considered. However, instead of insisting on my point of view, I decide to step back and listen carefully to his perspective. I realized that he was mainly worried about the workload distribution, not rejecting my ideas. After we clarified that, I suggested a new plan that combined both of our ideas, and it worked well. In the end, our collaboration improved a lot, and the project went smoothly. This experience taught me that active listening and clam communication are the best ways to handle disagreements.
