A few weeks ago, I had a disagreement with a friend about how to organize a group project for university. We both had different ideas about priorities and time management, and the conversation became tense.
Instead of reacting emotionally, I decided to take a short break and then suggested that we meet to discuss things calmly. During that talk, I tried to listen carefully to her point of view and explained mine without interrupting.
In the end, we found a compromise that worked for both of us — she managed the research part, and I focused on the presentation. It taught me how important it is to communicate openly and to separate emotions from the actual problem.
