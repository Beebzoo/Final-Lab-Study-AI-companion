Recently, I had a bit of a frustrating situation during a group project at university. One of the group members wasn’t really contributing much, but at the same time, they would disagree with decisions the rest of us had already agreed on. It started to get to me because it felt unfair and was slowing us down.

Instead of reacting in the moment, I decided to talk to them privately. I asked if everything was okay and if there was something making it hard for them to participate. That conversation actually helped a lot they admitted they were struggling to understand the work and felt a bit left out when things moved too fast.

After that, I suggested we slow things down a bit as a group and make sure everyone gets a chance to share their thoughts before making decisions. I also offered to help them with the parts they didn’t understand.

Things got a lot better after that. They started contributing more, and the group worked more smoothly. It made me realise that sometimes people aren’t being difficult on purpose they might just need a bit of support and better communication.
