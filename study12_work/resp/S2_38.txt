Sure. A recent challenging interaction I had was during a group project. One teammate wasn’t completing their parts, and it started to create tension because the rest of us were falling behind.

Instead of calling them out in front of everyone, I reached out privately to ask if something was going on. It turned out they were dealing with personal issues and struggling to keep up. I suggested we adjust our plan, I took on part of their task, and they focused on research instead. We also set clearer mini-deadlines and check-ins so everyone stayed on track.

By approaching it with empathy instead of frustration, we avoided conflict and finished the project on time. I learned that calm, direct communication usually works better than confrontation and that flexibility can really help a team get back on track.
