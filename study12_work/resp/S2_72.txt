I have to tell you, that just last month, my group for a major class project was about to completely fall apart. We had this one teammate who was all about style and trendy graphics, while the rest of us were very focused on the data and evidence. She thought we were boring, and we thought she was ignoring the actual assignment.
Instead of just making it worse, I decided to flip the narrative. I stopped the argument and asked her, 'Okay, forget the slides for a second. What's the one feeling you want the audience to have when we're done?'
She said she wanted them to be impressed and to remember our work. That was the breakthrough. I told her, 'Perfect. Let's use your incredible design skills to make our data unforgettable.' It immediately solves the situation.
We stopped fighting about the project and started collaborating on it. We ended up using her amazing visual style with our precise research, and honestly, we crushed it. It went from a potential disaster to our team's biggest win.
