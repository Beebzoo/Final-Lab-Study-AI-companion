I had a group project where one teammate kept skipping meetings and turning in their work late, then tried to blame the rest of us. It stressed me out a lot, but instead of getting angry, I asked to talk to them privately. I explained how I felt about the situation using calm “I” statements, so it didn’t sound like I was attacking them. When I listened, I found out they were going through some personal problems, which helped me understand their side. Together we agreed on smaller deadlines and regular check-ins so we could all stay on track. After that, things improved — they contributed more, we finished the project on time, and our grade didn’t suffer. From this, I learned that staying calm, communicating directly, and setting clear expectations really helps fix conflicts without making things worse.
