I had a situation recently where I disagreed with a friend about how we were handling a group project. We were both responsible for different parts, but they kept making changes to my section without telling me, which messed up the flow of what I had written. At first, I was frustrated and wanted to call them out directly, but I took a step back and messaged them instead, asking if we could talk about the edits they made.

When we talked, I made sure to start by acknowledging that I appreciated their effort and attention to detail, but I also explained that changing things without a discussion made it harder for me to keep things consistent. They admitted they just wanted to make the project better but didn’t realize it might come off as stepping on my work. After that, we agreed to check in with each other before making changes, and it actually made things run more smoothly.

It was a good reminder that addressing issues calmly and focusing on solutions works way better than reacting out of frustration.
