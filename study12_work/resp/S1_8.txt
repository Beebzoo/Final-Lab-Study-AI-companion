Hmm, okay. One recent challenging interaction I had was with a group member in a class project. We were working on a presentation together, and one person kept missing deadlines and wasn’t really communicating with the rest of us. I felt frustrated because it felt like we were picking up the slack, and I didn’t want our grade to suffer.

I decided to message them privately instead of calling them out in the group chat. I just said something like, “Hey, I’ve noticed you haven’t been able to contribute as much lately. Is everything okay? Let us know how we can help or adjust the workload if you’re going through something.”

Turns out they were dealing with some personal stuff and felt overwhelmed, but they didn’t want to ask for help. After that, we made a plan to divide the work a little differently, and they really stepped up. It reminded me how important it is to check in with people and assume good intent before jumping to conclusions.

It was definitely awkward at first, but I’m glad I handled it calmly and with empathy.
