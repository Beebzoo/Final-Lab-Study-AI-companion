A few weeks ago, I had a pretty tense moment with a coworker at my job at CVS. I’m a pharmacy technician, and we were both working a busy shift when a misunderstanding happened. I had filled a prescription and placed it in the bin, but my coworker thought it hadn’t been checked properly and started questioning me in front of a customer. I felt frustrated because I had done everything right, and I didn’t appreciate being called out like that in public.

But instead of snapping or getting defensive, I took a breath and said, “I understand you’re trying to make sure everything is accurate, but I already double-checked that one.” After our shift calmed down, I pulled her aside and explained how the way she approached me made me feel disrespected. I also asked if we could try to communicate differently next time—like talking privately first, instead of in front of patients.

She actually apologized, and we both agreed to be more mindful going forward. It wasn’t easy, but I’m glad I handled it maturely and didn’t let my emotions get the best of me. It taught me that staying calm and clear can really make a difference, even in high-pressure situations.
