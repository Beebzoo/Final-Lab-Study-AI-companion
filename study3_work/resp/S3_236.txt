I recently had an issue with a colleague who is slightly junior to me and who had not quite hit the mark in terms of the tasks that they were being set. And so they were offered some support by me, which they refused. They said they were fine. They were offered support from the department and they again said that they were fine. And so they were left to get on with it and to follow their processes. However, it became apparent that they were getting further and further behind. It then became necessary for me to step in as their line manager and to explain the situation to them and to furthermore give some constructive support as to how they could maximize their efficiencies. But also to allot some support from within the department to them. Otherwise, there was a strong likelihood that they would not consider hitting their targets within the time frame that was allowed. The junior member of staff who I've known for some time took this almost as a personal slight and tried to explain that they had lots of things on their plate. However, the only thing that was essential at the time was to get the tasks completed because other pieces of work depended on those completions being done. And it wasn't a personal slight on the junior member of staff. It was more that they needed to be supported so that they could achieve the tasks that had been set for them. There was no ambiguity. There was no nastiness. It was just a case of the tasks had to be completed and therefore support had to be given. There was no ill will towards the member of staff and there was no continuing resentment on my part towards the member of staff. However, for some time they tended not to come within earshot of me for fear that I may say something of which was never going to be the case.
