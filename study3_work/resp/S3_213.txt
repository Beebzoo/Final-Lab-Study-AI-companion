So, for me, a recent challenging interaction has been a new manager at work. They have a completely different way of working and from what I understand, everybody on the team is really struggling with it. They are not keen on positive feedback. Their idea really is as simple as if I don't comment, if I don't give you any feedback, then it's good. Whereas, I think our team in general is used to it, expects and likes a mixture of good feedback and constructive feedback on how to improve, but also be told where things are working. So, we got our quarterly feedback and there was nothing positive in there, just negativity. So, we had a meeting with our manager and we told them, look, this is how we work, this is how we like to work and this is how we grow best. For us to improve, we need to hear what we're doing well, but equally what's not working. It's a mixture of both. So, in this case, that's what we requested and the next feedback round that we got was better. It still wasn't great. What we were hoping for was feedback, not just in the quarterly formal feedback periods, but more sort of you hand over some work and they go, this is good, well done, fix this, this and this, but overall good. So, there's still progress to be made, but it's going in the right direction and I think this open, honest feedback between both parties is going to be what we need.
