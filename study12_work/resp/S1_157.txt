Alright, so one recent challenging interaction I had was during a group project in my finance class. We were working on a cost analysis case, and one of my teammates wasn’t pulling their weight. They kept missing deadlines and giving vague responses whenever we asked for updates. It was frustrating because we all depended on each other’s contributions to complete the project on time. At first, I didn’t want to be confrontational, but I also knew that ignoring the issue would just make things worse. So, I decided to approach the teammate privately. I kept my tone calm and started by acknowledging that I understood they might be busy with other coursework. Then, I directly asked if they were struggling with any part of the project. They admitted that they weren’t confident in their calculations and didn’t want to submit something incorrect. Once I understood their perspective, I suggested we go over the work together so I could help clarify any doubts. That way, they felt more supported, and we could make progress without resentment building up in the group. After that conversation, they were much more engaged, and we managed to finish our project on time. It reminded me how important it is to address issues early and approach conflicts with a problem-solving mindset rather than frustration.
