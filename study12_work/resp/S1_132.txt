Well, recently, I had a challenging interaction with a colleague at work. We were assigned to collaborate on a project, and we had different approaches to how we should tackle the task. I tend to be more structured and prefer t
o plan things out step by step, but my colleague is more spontaneous and likes to dive right in and adjust as we go along. At first, it caused some tension because I felt like we weren’t making any solid progress, and it seemed like we were always going in circles. I was frustrated, and I could tell they were getting frustrated with me too.

To handle the situation, I decided to be upfront but also calm. I asked if we could take a step back and talk about our work styles and how we could find a middle ground. I explained how I felt about the lack of structure and how it was making it harder for me to stay on track. I also listened to their side and realized that my approach was making them feel restricted, which wasn’t my intention.

In the end, we agreed on a compromise where we could stick to a loose plan but still leave room for flexibility. It wasn’t perfect, but it definitely helped improve the flow of the project and our communication. It felt good to be open about how I was feeling and to find a solution together.
