Recently, I had a challenging interaction with a colleague who had a very different approach to completing a project. They preferred to move quickly and make adjustments later, while I wanted to ensure we had accurate data and a clear plan before moving forward. To resolve the disagreement, I scheduled a meeting to discuss our priorities and understand each other’s perspectives. I listened to their concerns about time pressure and shared my reasoning for focusing on accuracy. We ultimately agreed to start with a small pilot test, allowing us to make progress quickly while still validating our data. This approach helped us identify potential issues early, stay on schedule, and strengthen our working relationship through better communication and mutual respect.
